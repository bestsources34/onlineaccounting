Records from ancient Persia show that governments implemented a fantastic audit system hundreds of years ago; receipts were written out for tax purposes which was an very early kind of bookkeeping.
Roman Realm:.

During the reign of Emperor Augustus, detailed governmental financial records were maintained which can be viewed as an very early form of public accounting.
Middle Ages:.

With the development of trade throughout the middle ages period in the Middle East and Europe, extra complex bookkeeping methods emerged.
Jewish areas are known to have actually utilized double-entry bookkeeping from the early-medieval period.
Italian vendors introduced accounting methods that included journals with credit ratings and debits.
Luca Pacioli-- The Father of Audit:.

In 1494 Luca Pacioli, an Italian mathematician and Franciscan friar, wrote "Summa de arithmetica," which included a section outlining the principles behind double-entry bookkeeping-- marking a substantial advancement in modern audit methods.
Industrial Revolution:.

The surge of commercial businesses needed much more innovative methods to keep track of monetary deals which better refined bookkeeping techniques.
This era saw the birth place of professional bookkeeping bodies such as Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales formed in 1880.
