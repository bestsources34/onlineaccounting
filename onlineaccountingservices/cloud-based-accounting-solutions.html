Audit, usually described as accountancy, is the methodical and detailed recording of financial purchases referring to a company. The background of accountancy dates back a number of thousand years and has actually developed considerably from its old beginnings.

During the regime of Emperor Augustus, detailed governmental economic documents were maintained which can be seen as an very early form of public audit.
Center Ages:.

With the development of profession during the middle ages duration in the center East and Europe, much more intricate audit methods arised.

Italian vendors introduced accounting methods that consisted of journals with credits and debits.

In 1494 Luca Pacioli, an Italian mathematician and Franciscan friar, wrote "Summa de arithmetica," which included a section detailing the principles behind double-entry accounting-- noting a considerable development in modern-day accountancy methods.

The increase of commercial companies required extra innovative methods to track monetary purchases which even more improved bookkeeping techniques.
